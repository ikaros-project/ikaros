## Year in Brief

2023 is a sparse year with eight active weeks. The work is targeted: input naming, data/vision, math/kernel, robotics, tests, and build/logging updates. Its importance is continuity: the repository remained tended even without the broad development waves seen in 2022 or 2024. It also prevents the devlog from implying that low-activity years are unimportant; maintenance years protect the work of high-activity years.

## Development Notes

The year starts with an input-name update and a short run of data/vision and math/kernel work. These commits keep the repository aligned and maintainable without opening a large new development front. These early updates kept names, data paths, and kernel assumptions from becoming stale. This kind of small alignment work reduces friction for the active maintenance recorded in the year.

Robotics and tests appear later in the year. The weekly logs show focused robotics updates and test/robotics combinations, suggesting maintenance of existing experimental setups rather than a large new module family. That makes 2023 a useful maintenance year, especially for robotics and tests that depend on external setups remaining reproducible. The suggested robotics/test image should therefore be practical and understated, matching the year’s character.

Build and logging updates close the active year. The project state at the end of 2023 is stable but quiet: Ikaros continues to receive targeted fixes and integration work, with no major thematic overhaul. The year is best summarized as a stabilization pause, with enough activity to keep the project coherent while larger work waited. That pause is part of the project’s real history and belongs in the annual sequence.
